## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem sellers face:** Choosing an agent feels like a gamble. Most agents look the same on paper. Sellers worry about overpricing, sitting on market, accepting a lowball offer, or being locked into a bad agent with no exit.

**Core problem buyers face:** The market moves fast, they're competing against other buyers, and their agent may not have the skills to win — or the financing knowledge to structure the strongest offer.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **Zillow-referred agents** — anonymous, volume-focused, no personal accountability
- **Large national teams** — hand-offs to junior agents, you never talk to the person you hired
- **Discount brokerages** — limited marketing, no network, you get what you pay for
- **Part-time or single agents** — no backup, limited bandwidth, variable expertise

**What it costs them:**
- Wrong agent = wrong price = months on market = price reductions = stigma
- Buyers without strategy = losing multiple offers = paying over asking unnecessarily
- Complex situations (divorce, probate) with unqualified agents = legal exposure, delays, family conflict

**Emotional tension:** "What if I choose wrong and it costs me tens of thousands of dollars?" / "What if we miss out on the right home again?"

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors:**
- Other local real estate teams in Oakland/Macomb/Wayne counties
- Individual top-producing agents in Southeast Michigan

**Secondary competitors:**
- Zillow Premier Agents — different distribution, same service promise
- Redfin — tech-forward, discount commission, lower touch
- Large RE/MAX, Keller Williams, or EXP mega-teams

**Indirect competitors:**
- iBuyers (Opendoor, etc.) — instant cash, but below-market price
- FSBO (For Sale By Owner) — no commission, but no expertise, marketing, or network
- Sitting on the decision — "we'll wait until next year"

**How each falls short:**
- Zillow agents: no relationship, no accountability, algorithm-matched not experience-matched
- Large teams: you hire the brand, get the junior agent
- Discount brokers: save on commission, lose on net proceeds (less marketing = lower offers)
- iBuyers: convenient but sellers typically net less after fees than with a skilled agent

| "I already know an agent" | Most clients who call us said the same thing first. We earn business because results speak — compare our track record, our marketing, and our guarantee before you decide. |
